5.1 American Enterprise Institute (AEI)

Foundation & Structure:

  • Founded: 1997 by William Kristol and Robert Kagan
  • Type: Neoconservative think tank based in Washington, D.C.
  • Mission: "Free markets and limited government" (official)
  • Status: Active (disbanded PNAC in 2006, but AEI continued operations)

Documented Funding Sources:

  • Charles Koch Foundation: >$1.6 million (2011–2016)
  • Donors Trust & Donors Capital Fund: >$1 million (2012–2016)
  • Sarah Scaife Foundation: >$1.7 million (2012–2016)
  • Bradley Foundation: $480,000 (2012–2016)
  • Coors Foundation: $425,000 (2011–2016)
  • Taiwan government: $550,000 contribution (undisclosed, revealed 2015)
  • Total documented (2003–2010): >$5.3 million

2013 Leak - "Schedule of Contributors":

  • Listed right-wing funders: Donors Capital Fund, Kern Family Foundation, Chamber of Commerce
  • Disclosed: $550,000 donation from de facto Taiwanese embassy
  • AEI figures (Daniel Blumenthal, Gary Schmitt) agitated for U.S. policy toward Taiwan

Funding Transparency:

  • AEI: "Operations are financed by donations from individuals, foundations, and corporations"
  • Does not accept government grants
  • Insists: "The Institute also does not accept any funding from foreign governments"

Policy Positions:

Foreign Policy - Pro-Israel:

  • NGO Report (2025): "AEI's funding primarily comes from conservative foundations, private donors, and pro-Israel philanthropists who share institute's mission to support free-market policies and robust U.S.-Israel relations."
  • SourceWatch (2001): "Coalition of willing" going to war with Iraq — strongly promotes right-wing "pro-Israel" position
  • Militarist Monitor (2019): AEI scholars exhibit "distinctly neoconservative edge and right-wing 'pro-Israel' bias, particularly with respect to U.S. policy on Iran and Israeli-Palestinian conflict"
  • AEI Middle East Archive (2022): "Iran is watching a growing disorder at the very top of both Democratic and Republican parties, and believe United States is too consumed with its own political dogfights to back Israel or intervene in case of an escalating war"
  • AEI Op-Eds: Multiple articles advocating for military action against Iran

Climate Policy - Denial:

  • Desmog (2025): "AEI was sixth on a list of organizations that received the highest climate skeptic funding between 2011 and 2013, reaching $5.7 million in total"

Notable Figures:

  • Daniel Blumenthal: AEI scholar, advocate for hardline on Iran, Syria
  • Gary Schmitt: AEI scholar, neoconservative foreign policy
  • John Yoo: Legal scholar, associated with torture memo authorship
  • Paul Wolfowitz: Former Defense official, PNAC signatory
