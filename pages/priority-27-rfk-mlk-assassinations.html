RFK AND MLK ASSASSINATIONS

Overview

The assassinations of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. (April 4, 1968, Memphis) and Senator Robert F. Kennedy (June 5, 1968, Los Angeles) occurred within two months of each other, effectively eliminating two of the most significant progressive leaders of the 1960s. Both assassinations came amidst FBI surveillance and harassment (for King) and at moments when political momentum was building toward transformative change. While official narratives identified lone gunmen—James Earl Ray for King, Sirhan Sirhan for Kennedy—significant questions persist about potential conspiracies, intelligence agency involvement, and broader political context.

Key Finding: Both assassinations occurred against leaders challenging establishment power structures, with FBI actively surveilling and seeking to discredit King through COINTELPRO. The timing—King during Poor People's Campaign, Kennedy on path to Democratic nomination—eliminated transformative political momentum. The speed of official conclusions (lone gunmen) contrasted with unresolved questions creates legitimate suspicion of broader involvement.

1. Martin Luther King Jr. Assassination

April 4, 1968 Timeline

Lorraine Motel, Memphis:

  • King staying at Lorraine Motel while organizing sanitation workers' strike
  • Room 306 on balcony
  • Approximately 6:01 PM CST: Single shot strikes King in neck/face
  • King pronounced dead at St. Joseph's Hospital at 7:05 PM

Investigation:

  • Police traced shot to bathroom window across street from rooming house
  • Found rifle abandoned
  • White Mustang seen fleeing scene

James Earl Ray

Official Narrative:

  • Born March 10, 1928, in Alton, Illinois
  • Criminal history: Robbery, prison escape
  • Used alias Eric Starvo Galt to purchase rifle
  • Rented room across from Lorraine Motel
  • Fled to Canada, then London, England
  • Arrested June 8, 1968, at Heathrow Airport

Confession:

  • March 10, 1969: Ray pleaded guilty to avoid death penalty
  • Sentenced to 99 years in prison
  • Later recanted confession, claiming he was scapegoat for larger conspiracy

Conspiracy Theories

Key Questions:

  • How did Ray, with limited resources and no prior history of political violence, execute professional assassination?
  • Why were King's movements and security minimal despite known threats?
  • Why did Memphis Police Department fail to provide adequate security?

Alleged Conspirators:

  • FBI: Given extensive surveillance and harassment of King
  • Local law enforcement: Memphis police potentially infiltrated
  • Organized crime: Possible connections through Ray's criminal background
  • Government intelligence: CIA or other agencies seeking to eliminate civil rights leader

1993 Loyd Jowers Confession:

  • Memphis restaurant owner claimed in 1993 that he participated in conspiracy to kill King
  • Claimed unnamed conspirator paid $100,000 to arrange killing
  • 1999 civil jury found Jowers and unnamed conspirators "liable" for King's death
  • Justice Department rejected findings due to insufficient evidence

HSCA Findings (1979)

House Select Committee on Assassinations:

  • Investigated King assassination along with RFK
  • Found "no evidence" of government conspiracy

2. FBI Surveillance of King: COINTELPRO

COINTELPRO Operations (1956-1971)

Full Name: Counter Intelligence Program
Objective: "Surveilling, infiltrating, discrediting, and disrupting American political parties and organizations that FBI perceived as subversive"

Targeting of King:

  • Started in 1956, during Montgomery bus boycott
  • Intensified through 1960s as King gained national prominence
  • Wiretaps, surveillance, informants

FBI Tactics Against King

Surveillance:

  • Wiretapping of King's phones
  • Bugging of hotel rooms
  • Infiltration of Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC)
  • Monitoring of meetings and travel

Discreditation Campaign:

  • November 1964: FBI sent anonymous letter to King suggesting he commit suicide before Nobel Peace Prize award
  • Distributed information about King's extramarital affairs (no evidence of Communist influence found)
  • Labeled King as "most dangerous and effective Negro leader"

The "Suicide Letter" (November 1964):

  • "You are done. There is but one way out for you. You better take it before your filthy, abnormal fraudulent self is bared to the nation."
  • Accompanied by tape recordings of King's affairs

Chronology Note: The suicide letter was sent to King just as he was becoming internationally recognized leader, representing explicit attempt to psychologically destroy him.

King's FBI Criticisms

1967 Address:

  • King condemned FBI for "failing to aid in civil rights fight"
  • Criticized FBI focus on Communist accusations rather than addressing racism
  • Called for congressional oversight of FBI

FBI Response:

  • Increased surveillance intensity
  • Internal memos discussed "neutralizing" King
  • Director J. Edgar Hoover expressed personal animosity toward King

Surveillance at Time of Assassination

FBI Presence:

  • FBI informant in Memphis (Lloyd Jowers)
  • FBI had five paid informants within Memphis Police Department
  • Single informant began surveilling King in February 1968, just two months before assassination
  • Informant was present in motel courtyard when King was assassinated

Chronology Analysis: The presence of FBI informants and surveillance immediately surrounding King's assassination raises questions about agency knowledge or potential involvement.

3. Robert F. Kennedy Assassination

June 5, 1968 Timeline

Ambassador Hotel, Los Angeles:

  • Kennedy celebrated California Democratic primary victory (won June 4)
  • Midnight speech to supporters in Embassy Room
  • Approximately 12:15 AM: Kennedy left through pantry area
  • Multiple shots fired
  • Kennedy hit multiple times, including fatal wound to head
  • Kennedy died 25 hours later at Good Samaritan Hospital

Sirhan Sirhan

Official Narrative:

  • Born March 19, 1944, in Jerusalem, Palestine
  • Christian Arab, immigrant to United States
  • Motive: Kennedy's support for Israel in June 1967 Six-Day War
  • Sirhan believed Kennedy "deliberately betrayed" Palestinians
  • Found with .22 revolver in pantry
  • Convicted and sentenced to death (commuted to life)

Witness Discrepancies:

  • Witnesses placed Sirhan standing in pantry, 3-5 feet from Kennedy
  • Wound trajectory suggested shots from behind Kennedy
  • Powder burns on Kennedy's ear suggested closer range than Sirhan's position
  • Multiple witnesses reported "girl in polka dot dress" potentially distracting Sirhan

Acoustic and Forensic Questions

Gunshot Count:

  • Kennedy struck by multiple bullets
  • Several bystanders also wounded
  • Official account: Sirhan's 8-shot revolver
  • Questions about whether more shots fired than Sirhan's weapon capacity

Autopsy Findings:

  • Fatal shot entered behind right ear
  • Trajectory suggested shooter behind Kennedy
  • Contradicts Sirhan's position facing Kennedy in pantry

Chronology Note: Ballistic and trajectory evidence creates questions about whether Sirhan fired all shots, particularly fatal wound.
