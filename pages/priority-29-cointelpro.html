COINTELPRO

Overview

COINTELPRO (Counter Intelligence Program) was FBI's secret program to surveil, infiltrate, discredit, and disrupt domestic political organizations and individuals deemed "subversive" from 1956 to 1971. Operating under J. Edgar Hoover's leadership, COINTELPRO targeted civil rights leaders, anti-war activists, Black Power organizations, feminist groups, and others challenging establishment power structures. The program's existence remained secret until 1971 Church Committee revelations, though tactics continued under different names.

Key Finding: COINTELPRO represented systematic government campaign against domestic political dissent, targeting individuals and organizations challenging racial hierarchy, militarism, and social injustice. The program's scope—targeting virtually every significant social movement of 1950s and 1960s—demonstrated FBI acting as political police force protecting establishment interests from democratic challenges.
