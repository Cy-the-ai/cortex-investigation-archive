9/11 INVESTIGATION

Overview

The September 11, 2001 attacks represent the defining event of the early 21st century, catalyzing the War on Terror, enabling unprecedented surveillance expansion through the USA PATRIOT Act, and providing justification for military interventions across the Middle East. While the official narrative identifies 19 al-Qaeda hijackers and Osama bin Laden as responsible, significant questions persist about intelligence failures, NORAD response, the collapse of World Trade Center buildings, and potential foreknowledge among U.S. officials. The aftermath of 9/11 witnessed the rapid implementation of policies that neoconservative think tanks had advocated for years prior, suggesting pre-existing agendas that found enabling conditions in the crisis.

Key Finding: The 9/11 attacks cannot be understood as an isolated intelligence failure but as a convergence of missed warnings, delayed response, and pre-existing political agendas that found in the attacks an opportunity to implement policies (Patriot Act, Iraq War) that had been previously advocated but lacked political support. The anthrax attacks that followed targeted Democratic senators and media figures, creating additional pressure to pass legislation that expanded state power.

1. The Official Narrative and Timeline

The Attacks

September 11, 2001 Timeline:

Morning:

  • 7:59 AM: American Airlines Flight 11 departs Boston Logan International Airport
  • 8:14 AM: Flight 11 hijacked
  • 8:46 AM: Flight 11 crashes into North Tower of World Trade Center
  • 9:03 AM: United Airlines Flight 175 crashes into South Tower of World Trade Center

Morning - Pentagon and Pennsylvania:

  • 9:37 AM: American Airlines Flight 77 crashes into Pentagon
  • 9:59 AM: South Tower collapses (56 minutes after impact)
  • 10:03 AM: United Airlines Flight 93 crashes in Shanksville, Pennsylvania
  • 10:28 AM: North Tower collapses (1 hour, 42 minutes after impact)

Afternoon:

  • 5:20 PM: World Trade Center Building 7 collapses

Casualties:

  • 2,977 victims killed in the attacks (excluding hijackers)
  • 246 killed on four aircraft
  • 2,606 killed in World Trade Center and surrounding area
  • 125 killed at Pentagon

Official Attribution: The 9/11 Commission Report identified 19 al-Qaeda hijackers, with Osama bin Laden as the operational mastermind.

Hijackers:

  • 15 Saudi nationals
  • 2 United Arab Emirates nationals
  • 1 Egyptian national
  • 1 Lebanese national

Al-Qaeda Connection: Bin Laden's 1998 and 2002 video messages claimed responsibility for the attacks.

3. NORAD Response and the Stand Down Controversy

NORAD's Mission and Capabilities

NORAD (North American Aerospace Defense Command):

  • Joint U.S.-Canada organization for aerospace warning and control
  • Traditionally focused on external threats (Soviet bombers, cruise missiles)
  • Capabilities for intercepting aircraft within U.S. territory limited pre-9/11

Pre-9/11 Configuration:

  • 14 alert sites with fighters on ready alert
  • 4-5 minutes notice for fighters to scramble
  • Interception protocols based on Cold War threat models

The NORAD Timeline

Official Timeline (9/11 Commission Report):

  • 8:37 AM: FAA notifies NORAD about Flight 11 hijacking
  • 8:46 AM: Flight 11 impacts North Tower
  • 9:03 AM: Flight 175 impacts South Tower (before fighters launched)
  • 9:37 AM: Flight 77 impacts Pentagon (fighters airborne but not yet in position)
  • 10:03 AM: Flight 93 crashes in Pennsylvania

Timeline Discrepancies:

  • NORAD issued revised timeline in September 2001
  • FAA notification times adjusted later after examination of records
  • Original timeline suggested more time for interception

The Stand Down Theory

Conspiracy Claim: NORAD issued a stand down order or deliberately delayed response to allow hijacked aircraft to reach targets.

Arguments Supporting Theory:

  • NORAD had successfully intercepted off-course aircraft previously (e.g., Payne Stewart's plane in 1999)
  • Response time on 9/11 significantly slower than standard protocols
  • Multiple military exercises on 9/11 (Vigilant Guardian, Global Guardian) created confusion
  • Delayed fighter launches despite early notification

9/11 Commission Rebuttal:

  • NORAD perceived dominant threat as external (cruise missiles), not domestic hijackings
  • No domestic radar coverage comparable to external warning systems
  • Communications difficulties between FAA and military
  • Exercises on 9/11 reduced available fighter aircraft but did not cause failures

NORAD Tapes:

  • NORAD audio tapes from 9/11 differ from official timeline in some respects
  • Some tapes suggest earlier awareness of hijackings than acknowledged
  • Discrepancies fuel conspiracy allegations about deliberate delay

Exercise Confusion

9/11 Military Exercises:

  • Vigilant Guardian: NORAD exercise simulating hijacked aircraft in Northeast U.S.
  • Global Guardian: Strategic Command exercise
  • National Reconnaissance Office exercise: Simulating aircraft crashing into building

Impact:

  • Confusion between real events and exercise scenarios
  • Reduced availability of fighters for actual response
  • Radar analysts filtering real planes as exercise participants

Chronology Analysis: The presence of multiple exercises on 9/11 has been noted as either:

  • Coincidence that reduced response capability
  • Intentional confusion mechanism to facilitate attacks (conspiracy view)
  • Evidence of bureaucratic overcomplexity and poor coordination (official view)

4. World Trade Center Collapse Theories

Official Explanation

Twin Towers Collapse Mechanism (NIST Report):

  • Aircraft impact severed critical structural columns
  • Jet fuel fires ignited office materials, creating intense heat
  • Steel weakened from heat, leading to progressive collapse
  • Floor trusses sagged from heat, pulling columns inward
  • Structural columns eventually buckled under combined gravity and lateral loads

Building 7 Collapse (NIST Report):

  • Not hit by aircraft
  • Fires ignited by debris from North Tower collapse
  • Uncontrolled fires burned for approximately 7 hours
  • Thermal expansion of steel beams caused critical column failure
  • Progressive collapse at 5:20 PM

Controlled Demolition Theories

Architects and Engineers for 9/11 Truth:

  • Claims over 2,400 architects and engineers as members
  • Argues that collapse characteristics resemble controlled demolition
  • Points to:
  • Freefall acceleration of Building 7 (NIST acknowledged 2.25 seconds of freefall)
  • Symmetrical collapse despite asymmetric damage
  • Presence of molten metal in debris
  • Sulfidation of steel (indicating thermite/thermate)
  • Explosive sounds reported by witnesses

Twin Towers Demolition Claims:

  • Squibs (puffs of smoke) seen below collapse front
  • Pyroclastic flows (expanding dust clouds) characteristic of demolition
  • Near freefall descent speed
  • Complete pulverization of concrete and steel

NIST Responses:

  • Freefall in Building 7 limited to early phase of collapse
  • Symmetrical collapse consistent with column failure sequence
  • Molten metal from aluminum and other building materials
  • No evidence of explosives or demolition charges

Building 7 Specifics

Characteristics Fueling Theories:

  • Not hit by aircraft
  • Fires from North Tower debris
  • Unprecedented complete collapse of steel-framed skyscraper from fire alone
  • Larry Silverstein statement: "maybe the smartest thing to do is pull it" (interpreted by some as demolition order)

Silverstein Comments:

  • "I said, you know, we've had such terrible loss of life, maybe the smartest thing to do is pull it. And they made that decision to pull and we watched the building collapse."
  • Silverstein claimed "pull it" referred to withdrawing firefighters
  • Demolition proponents argue "pull" is demolition industry term for controlled demolition

Chronology Note: Building 7 collapsed approximately 7 hours after Twin Towers collapse, with relatively modest fires compared to earlier buildings. The extended delay between impact and collapse provided opportunity for preparation if demolition was planned.

5. The Patriot Act and Legislative Rush

The USA PATRIOT Act

Full Name: Uniting and Strengthening America by Providing Appropriate Tools Required to Intercept and Obstruct Terrorism Act

Enactment:

  • Introduced October 23, 2001
  • Passed House: 357-66 (October 24)
  • Passed Senate: 98-1 (October 25)
  • Signed by President Bush: October 26, 2001

Timeline Note: Passed just 45 days after 9/11, with minimal time for debate or amendment.

Key Provisions

Surveillance Expansion:

  • Section 215: "Business records" provision allowing collection of "any tangible things" with court order (basis for NSA metadata collection)
  • Roving Wiretaps: Surveillance of individuals rather than specific devices
  • Sneak and Peek: Delayed notification of searches
  • National Security Letters: Administrative subpoenas without judicial approval

Other Provisions:

  • Expanded definition of terrorism
  • Enhanced immigration enforcement authority
  • Money laundering and financial tracking
  • Information sharing between intelligence and law enforcement

Legislative Process

Rushed Debate:

  • Most members of Congress had not read the full legislation
  • Amendments to limit surveillance authority rejected
  • Minimal oversight or sunset provisions initially
  • Criticism of passing legislation in "times of fear"

Public Pressure:

  • Anthrax attacks targeting Democratic Senator Tom Daschle and media figures
  • Media environment demanding congressional action
  • Bush administration warnings that "the terrorists want us to stop being Americans"

Civil Liberties Opposition:

  • ACLU and civil liberties groups raised concerns
  • Some members of Congress expressed reservations
  • Most opposition silenced by political pressure
