1. ARCHAEOLOGICAL DATING METHODS & LIMITATIONS

1.1 Radiocarbon Dating: Systematic Uncertainties

Fundamental Limitations:

Assumption of 5730-Year Half-Life:

  • C14 half-life assumed constant at 5,730 years
  • Variation possible over time
  • Calibrated dates may differ "substantially" from best estimate of actual calendar date
  • Wikipedia: "When a date is quoted, reader should be aware that if it is an uncalibrated date (a term used for dates given in radiocarbon years) it may differ substantially from the best estimate of the actual calendar date"

Reservoir Effects:

Carbon Exchange Reservoirs:

  • Carbon distributed throughout atmosphere, biosphere, and oceans
  • Each component has different C14/C12 ratios
  • Ocean reservoir has different isotopic composition from atmosphere
  • Ancient samples from marine environments require correction for "reservoir effect"
  • C14.arch.ox.ac.uk: "because radiocarbon composition of oceans differs from that of the atmosphere, this can lead to erroneous dates"
  • Parks Canada: "Shellfish remains are common in coastal and estuarine archaeological sites, but dating these samples requires a correction for 'reservoir effect'"

Post-1950 Nuclear Testing:

  • Nuclear testing altered global C14 levels
  • Sciencenotes.org: "Post-1950 Contamination – Nuclear testing altered global 14C levels"
  • Carbon dating not reliable for samples formed after nuclear testing

Contamination Issues:

  • Modern carbon contamination affects ancient samples
  • Ancient samples vulnerable to handling contamination
  • Requires careful sample processing and storage

Dating Range Limitations:

Age Upper Limits:

  • Materials older than ~50,000 years have significant uncertainty
  • Multiple sources acknowledge this limitation
  • CIRAM Lab: "Find out when method becomes ineffective and which alternatives to choose depending on materials analyzed"

1.2 Alternative Dating Methods

Optically Stimulated Luminescence (OSL):

  • Dates last exposure of minerals to sunlight
  • Useful for ceramics and sediments
  • Radiocarbon.com: Alternative dating method with different assumptions

Dendrochronology:

  • Analysis of overlapping historical chronologies
  • Statistical methods to date tree rings and historical events
  • Used by some chronology researchers

Multi-Method Approaches:

  • "The dynamic approach includes ink, paper and toner ageing"
  • ScienceDirect article (2019): Methods include accelerated ageing techniques, chemometric approaches, constructing chronology of documents
  • Purpose: Cross-verify dates using multiple independent methods
