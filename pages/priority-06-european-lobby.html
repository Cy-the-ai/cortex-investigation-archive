2. NATIONAL ANTI-BDS LEGISLATION: CASE STUDIES

2.1 German Bundestag Resolution (May 17, 2019)

Resolution: "Resisting the BDS Movement"

  • First of its kind by major European parliament (Haaretz, 2019)
  • Cross-party alliance: CDU, SPD, FDP, Green Party supported
  • Definition: BDS labeled as anti-semitic for its "pattern of argument and methods"
  • Comparison: Compared BDS to Nazi era "Don't Buy" stickers and slogans calling for boycotting Jews
  • Action required:
  • German government urged to resolutely condemn all calls to boycott Israel
  • Not to support or finance organizations calling for boycott of Israel or supporting BDS
  • Strongly condemn campaigns against sale of Israeli products or boycotting prominent Israeli individuals
  • Welcome municipalities that refuse financial support to BDS movement

Failed Motions:

  • Far-right AfD motion calling for outlawing BDS (failed)
  • Die Linke motion condemning "anti-semitic incidences" in BDS movement (failed)

Political Impact:

  • Israeli Foreign Ministry welcomed Bundestag resolution
  • Stated: "German parliament has recognized anti-semitic nature of BDS and its unlawful boycott activities"
  • Frankfurt, Berlin, Munich said BDS uses language from Nazi era (DW, 2017)

2.2 France Anti-Zionism and Anti-Terrorism Laws
